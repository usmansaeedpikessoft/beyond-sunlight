08 Conclusion

Usage of NASA source Data

1.⁠ ⁠We based the formation of our water bodies on the following NASA resource: 'Ocean Worlds: Water in the Solar System and Beyond'. Gravity's gentle tug caused our planet's interior to heat up, melting ice and creating hidden oceans beneath the surface.
2.⁠ ⁠The decision to locate Thalassokhimia in an exoplanetary system was inspired by 'Ocean World Resources'. Drawing inspiration from NASA's research on oceanic celestial bodies, our design is modeled after the captivating moons of Europa and Enceladus.
3.⁠ ⁠We utilized open data from various non-NASA sources to research organisms that thrive through chemosynthesis on the planet Earth so that we could find samples for the organisms we were designing to flourish on Thalassokhimia. Our designed organisms are mostly found near hydrothermal vents on Earth or those hypothesized on Europa. These creatures can also thrive in dark, high-pressure environments, using energy from chemical reactions rather than sunlight.
