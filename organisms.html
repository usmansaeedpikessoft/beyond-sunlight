06 ORGANISMS

Northern Chemotrophs: The Basalt Spires

  1. Live in deep, icy oceans near the planet's north pole.
  2. Forms towering, columnar structures resembling black rocky forests.
  3. Extracts energy from sulfur and methane compounds from the planet's mantle.
  4. Covered in protective mineral layers for pressure and temperature resistance.
  5. Thrives in high-pressure, high-temperature environments.
  6. Found on the ocean floor, stretching towards the ice crust underside.
  7. Highly specialized root system for energy extraction.
  8. Endures extreme conditions in the absence of sunlight.

Crybobecteria

  1. A psychrophilic (cold-loving) bacterium that thrives near hydrothermal vents.
  2. It uses hydrogen sulfide from the vents to produce energy via chemosynthesis, withstanding extreme cold and pressure.
  3. Forms a microbial mat around the vents, providing the primary energy source for the local ecosystem.

Glacial Fern

  1. A hardy, root-like organism that grows around hydrothermal vents.
  2. It has no need for sunlight and instead absorbs minerals from the hydrothermal vent water, converting them into energy through chemosynthesis.
  3. Primary producer that sustains the ecosystem by releasing energy-rich compounds for primary consumers to feed on.

Vent Shrimper

  1. A shrimp-like creature adapted to feeding on Glacial Fern and microbial mats.
  2. Its tough, insulated exoskeleton protects it from both predators and extreme cold. Specialized appendages allow it to scrape bacterial mats and consume the ferns.
  3. Important herbivore and detritivore, regulating the growth of microbial and plant life in the polar region.

Glacial Stalker

  1. A large, predatory fish with bioluminescent spots.
  2. It uses bioluminescence to attract or confuse prey, primarily feeding on Vent Shrimpers. Its keen sense of detecting sulfur compounds helps it locate prey.
  3. Apex predator in the northern polar region, controlling the population of primary consumers and maintaining ecosystem stability.

Southern Chemotrophs: The Abyssal Webs

  1. Delicate, web-like entities spreading across the ocean floor.
  2. Anchored to rocky bed by mineral-coated tendrils.
  3. Extracts chemical energy from hydrogen and iron compounds.
  4. Supports ecosystem relying entirely on chemical energy.
  5. Thrives in complete darkness, lacking sunlight.
  6. Found in ocean sediments and rocky ocean floor.
  7. Supports dependent species feeding on waste products.

Methanocies

  1. DMethane-utilizing archaea that thrive in the cold methane seeps found near the southern pole.
  2. Converts methane into organic compounds through chemosynthesis.
  3. Provides the foundation for life near methane seeps, essential for all other species in the region.

Hydro Stalk

  1. A tubeworm-like chemosynthetic producer growing around methane seeps.
  2. Extracts hydrogen sulfide from the seep's water to sustain itself, with symbiotic bacteria in its tissues.
  3. Primary producer, converting methane and sulfides into energy, supporting an ecosystem based on chemical energy.

Seep Grazer

  1. A small, armored snail that feeds on Hydro Stalk.
  2. Its shell provides protection against predators and its radula allows it to scrape algae and other microbial growth from surfaces near methane seeps.
  3. Primary consumer that grazes on chemosynthetic producers, forming a vital link in the polar food web.

Abyssal Claw

  1. A deep-sea crustacean with sharp claws and a hardened exoskeleton.
  2. It has powerful claws to break open the shells of Seep Grazers and other small organisms. Its highly sensitive chemical receptors allow it to detect prey in the methane-rich waters.
  3. Apex predator of the southern polar region, playing a crucial role in controlling herbivore populations and promoting biodiversity.
