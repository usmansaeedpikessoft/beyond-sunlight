Red Dwarf Star

Thalassokhimia is an aquatic orbiting a red dwarf star, where organisms have evolved to adapt to the low-intensity, reddish light and other energy sources like volcanic activity., adapting to the dynamic and ever-changing environment. Over billions of years, life has thrived, combining chemosynthesis and adapted forms of photosynthesis. Thalassokhimia experiences two main seasons:

  1. Summer: The planet moves closer to the red dwarf star, allowing organisms to maximize light capture. During this period, photosynthetic organisms thrive in the upper ocean, creating an abundance of energy for herbivorous species and their predators.
  2. Winter: As the planet moves farther from the red dwarf, light levels drop even further, reducing photosynthetic activity. Many organisms retreat to deeper parts of the ocean, where volcanic activity becomes the primary energy source, and chemosynthesis dominates the ecosystem.
